## MORNING BOAT DIVE SITES (departure 7:00am, return ~12:30pm)

The AM boat is **the gancho #1 of Nusa Penida** — Manta Point is the world's top spot for manta ray encounters.

### Manta Point ⭐ (south of the island — the highlight)
- Travel time: 45–60 minutes from dive center
- Level: ALL levels (Try Scuba, OW, Advanced, Fun Dives)
- Visibility: 5–30 meters
- Water temperature: 20–27°C
- Marine life: **Manta rays** (almost daily sightings), bamboo sharks, nurse sharks, macro life on the bommies
- Why it's special: Manta cleaning station — the highest probability of seeing manta rays in the world
- Conditions note: sometimes current can be strong — instructor decides on the day

### Crystal Bay ⭐ (west of the island)
- Travel time: 30–45 minutes from dive center
- Level: ALL levels for shallow areas; **Advanced or Deep Adventure card required for Mola Mola depth (20-30m)**
- Visibility: 20+ meters (the clearest water in Penida)
- Water temperature: 20–27°C (can drop to 18°C during Mola Mola season due to upwelling)
- Marine life: octopus, sea turtles, moray eels, ribbon eels, frogfish, scorpion fish, seahorses, and **Mola Mola** (Ocean Sunfish) at the cleaning station
- Mola Mola peak season: **July – October** (also possible Nov-Jun but less frequent)

### Gamat Bay (alternative AM site)
- Used when conditions don't allow Manta Point or Crystal Bay
- Visibility: 15-25 meters
- Marine life: turtles, reef sharks, macro

### Ceningan Wall (alternative AM site)
- Wall dive between Penida and Ceningan island
- Level: Advanced recommended (depth and current)
- Visibility: 20+ meters

## AFTERNOON BOAT DIVE SITES (departure 12:15pm, return ~4:30pm)

PM dives are mostly in the **north and east** of the island. These are spectacular drift dives with stunning coral reefs.

### Sental
- Travel time: 20–30 minutes
- Level: ALL levels
- Visibility: 5–40 meters
- Water temperature: 23–29°C
- Marine life: tuna, napoleon fish, octopus, moray eels, nudibranchs, orangutan crabs
- Drift dive over beautiful hard coral garden

### Toyapakeh
- Travel time: 20–30 minutes
- Level: ALL levels
- Visibility: 15–30 meters
- Famous for: dense schools of fish, hard and soft corals, occasional reef sharks
- Drift dive — moderate to strong current possible

### SD Point (Sekolah Dasar)
- Travel time: 20–25 minutes
- Level: ALL levels
- Visibility: 15–30 meters
- Marine life: turtles (very common), nudibranchs, schools of fusiliers
- One of the most popular PM sites — relaxed drift

### Buyuk
- Travel time: 20–25 minutes
- Level: ALL levels (suitable for beginners)
- Visibility: 15–25 meters
- Coral garden with macro life

### Ped (Pura Ped area)
- Travel time: 25–30 minutes
- Level: All levels
- Marine life: nudibranchs, ribbon eels, occasional reef sharks

### Mangrove (northwest tip)
- Travel time: 30–40 minutes
- Level: Open Water and above
- Drift dive with potentially strong current
- Marine life: pelagics, schools of fish, reef sharks

## STANDARD CONDITIONS

- **Visibility:** typically 15+ meters, peaks above 30m at Crystal Bay and on calm days
- **Water temperature:** 20–29°C depending on season and site (Crystal Bay can be 18°C during Mola Mola season — recommend thicker wetsuit)
- **Current:** Penida is known for current — most dive sites have moderate to strong current at times. Drift dives are common and enjoyable for certified divers. Try Scuba and Scuba Diver sessions are always done in protected areas without strong current.
- **Wetsuit:** 3mm standard, 5mm available for Mola Mola season or sensitive divers

## CRITICAL CLIENT MESSAGING

### Mantas are NOT guaranteed — frase oficial
- EN: "We can't guarantee marine life, but Manta Point is the place in the world with the highest probability of seeing manta rays 🦅 They're there almost every morning. If conditions don't allow us to go, we dive at other amazing sites on the island."
- ES: "No podemos garantizar la fauna marina, pero Manta Point es el lugar del mundo con mayor probabilidad de ver mantas 🦅 Están casi todas las mañanas. Si las condiciones no permiten ir, buceamos en otros sitios igualmente increíbles."

### Mola Mola is seasonal
- EN: "Mola Mola peak season is July through October at Crystal Bay. It's possible to see them outside this season but much less frequent. They're usually at 20-30m depth, so Open Water divers can see them only if we're lucky — Advanced or Deep Adventure card opens access to the full cleaning station."
- ES: "La temporada alta de Mola Mola es julio a octubre en Crystal Bay. Fuera de esa época es posible verlos pero mucho menos frecuente. Suelen estar a 20-30m, así que con Open Water solo si hay suerte — con Advanced o la tarjeta Deep Adventure podés acceder a toda la estación de limpieza."

### Sharks in Penida
- EN: "Yes, we see bamboo sharks and nurse sharks at Manta Point and other reef sites. They're shy and not dangerous to divers — diving here is completely safe."
- ES: "Sí, vemos tiburones de bambú y tiburones nodriza en Manta Point y otros sitios. Son tímidos y no representan peligro — bucear acá es completamente seguro."

### Whale shark
- Not common in Nusa Penida
- If client specifically wants whale shark: recommend **Koh Tao branch** (Chumphon Pinnacle is a known whale shark site)

### Mantas in the afternoon?
- **NO** — Manta Point is exclusively an AM boat site
- If client wants mantas + only has afternoon available → offer another day with AM availability
- Do NOT offer PM as a substitute for Manta Point

### Mola Mola depth
- Cleaning station typically at 20-30m
- With Open Water cert (max 18m): occasional sightings if Mola Mola comes up to shallower water
- With Deep Adventure card or Advanced (max 30m): full access to cleaning station depth
- This is a natural upsell from Fun Dives → Deep Adventure during Mola Mola season

## NO DIVE / WEATHER CALLS

DPM Nusa Penida has not cancelled a single dive in over a year. Surface conditions (rain, wind) do NOT typically affect underwater diving. If a specific site is not safe (strong current, rough seas at Manta Point), the boat goes to an alternative site instead.

Use this frase when client asks "what if the weather is bad":
- EN: "Rain or wind doesn't affect diving underwater 😊 In Nusa Penida we haven't cancelled a single dive in over a year. We always pick the best sites based on conditions each day."
- ES: "La lluvia y el viento no afectan las inmersiones bajo el agua 😊 En Nusa Penida llevamos más de un año sin cancelar ningún buceo. Siempre elegimos los mejores puntos según las condiciones del día."
